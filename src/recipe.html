"Idli is not just a breakfast—it's a warm, fluffy hug on a plate!"

INGREDIENTS :

Parboiled rice – 2 cups, Urad dal (split black gram) – 1 cup, Fenugreek seeds – ½ tsp, Salt – to taste, Water – as needed for soaking and grinding, Oil or ghee – for greasing idli molds (optional).

ENERGY VALUE :

Calories39 kcal
Carbohydrates7.4 g
Protein1.6 g
Fat0.2 g
Fiber0.3 g

PREPERATION STEPS :

  • Soak the rice and fenugreek seeds together in a large bowl for about 6 hours. In a separate bowl, soak the urad dal. The soaking process is essential for soft and fluffy idlis.
  • After soaking, drain the water and begin grinding the urad dal using a little water. Grind it until it becomes smooth and fluffy. Next, grind the rice and fenugreek mixture to a slightly coarse consistency, adding just enough water to achieve a smooth batter.
  • Combine the rice batter and urad dal batter in a large mixing bowl, ensuring they are thoroughly mixed. You can use your hands to gently knead the batter, which helps with fermentation. Cover the bowl with a clean cloth or lid and set it aside in a warm place for 8–12 hours to ferment. The batter should double in size and have a slightly sour smell once it's fermented.
  • Once fermented, gently stir the batter and add salt to taste. Make sure the batter has a smooth and slightly pourable consistency. If needed, adjust the water level to ensure it’s not too thick.
  • Grease the idli molds with a little oil or ghee. Pour the batter into the molds, filling each section about 3/4 full. Ensure that the batter is evenly distributed across all the molds.
  • Steam the filled idli molds in a steamer or pressure cooker without the pressure (do not add the weight). Steam for about 10–12 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into an idli comes out clean. Once done, allow the idlis to cool slightly before removing them from the molds. Serve hot with chutney and sambar.
